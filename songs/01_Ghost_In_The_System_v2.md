## PRODUCTION NOTES:

• Key: F# minor — dark, tense, cinematic, slightly unusual (avoids the obvious Em/Am), accommodates baritone-to-tenor lift
• Tempo: 78 BPM (heartbeat pace, industrial mechanical feel)
• Time Signature: 4/4
• Chord Progression:
  Verse: i – VI – III – VII (F#m – D – A – E) [open, drifting, melancholy]
  Pre-Chorus: iv – i – VII – VII (Bm – F#m – E – E) [building, unresolved]
  Chorus: i – VI – VII – i (F#m – D – E – F#m) [circular, trapped]
  Bridge: iv – iv – i – VII (Bm – Bm – F#m – E) [static, then falling]
  Final Chorus: i – VI – VII – III (F#m – D – E – A) [wider, more desperate]
• Vocal: Male baritone (A2–B4) with tenor emotional lifts in chorus peaks
• Instruments: Ambient synth drone, minimal piano, industrial percussion (muted), sub bass, atmospheric pads, cinematic drums (chorus), electric guitar (atmospheric/reverb), glitch FX (progressive degradation)
• Dynamics: Ambient drone (intro) → sparse intimate (V1) → building pressure (PC) → wide cinematic (C1) → hollow/detached (V2) → heavier/distorted (C2) → stripped bare (bridge) → massive corrupted force (FC) → degrading fade (outro) → spoken robotic (transition)
• Hook Type: Dual hook — "Ghost in the system" (title chant) + "Vole-tek online / Human offline" (identity split statement)
• Rhyme Scheme: V=AABB (couplets — clean, tight, em-dash phrasing), PC=AABB, C=Free (emotional priority), Bridge=ABCB
• Emotional Arc: Burnout routine → automated numbness → identity dissolving → desperate question → corruption → the system claims him
