# Cat Jail — Rewritten v2

> Complete rewrite for stronger misdirect, tighter specificity, better hook mechanics,
> and more personality. Original had the right concept but generic phrasing.
> The key to this song: every line must GENUINELY sound romantic/suggestive until
> the reveal — then on re-listen, every line is OBVIOUSLY about a cat. Double meaning
> on EVERY line, not just the vibe.

---

## STYLE PROMPT:
Playful indie pop, quirky bedroom pop, light funk groove, conversational male vocal, warm and cheeky delivery, 104 BPM, G major, bright Rhodes keys, fingerpicked nylon guitar, soft bouncy bass, light programmed drums with rim clicks, handclaps on chorus, minimal airy production, catchy and loopable, modern indie vibes, warm but not saccharine, Vulfpeck meets Rex Orange County

Structure: short instrumental intro → talk-singing verse (suggestive misdirect) → bouncy pre-chorus → catchy repetitive chorus with la-di-da hook + punchline → verse 2 (deeper misdirect) → chorus → playful bridge (reveal energy) → final chorus (lean into humor) → soft tag outro

Vocal: relaxed male, slightly sarcastic, talk-singing, natural imperfections, conversational timing, deadpan delivery on punchline "I'm in cat jail", warm not cool, the guy next door not a pop star

Production: simple pocket groove, space between notes, rhythmic bounce from bass and keys, handclaps enter on chorus, keep it breathing and human, focus on timing and hook repetition, slightly lo-fi warmth

[Exclusions: ‑heavy autotune, ‑EDM drops, ‑aggressive energy, ‑dramatic vocals, ‑orchestral anything, ‑trap drums, ‑distortion, ‑dark atmosphere, ‑screaming, ‑beatboxing, ‑vocal hums]

## PRODUCTION NOTES:

• Key: G major — bright, warm, happy, guitar-friendly, playful energy
• Tempo: 104 BPM (bouncy walking pace, light funk pocket, not too fast)
• Time Signature: 4/4
• Chord Progression:
  Verse: I – iii – IV – V (G – Bm – C – D) [bright, forward-moving, classic pop]
  Pre-Chorus: IV – V – vi – V (C – D – Em – D) [slight tension before release]
  Chorus: I – V – vi – IV (G – D – Em – C) [earworm standard — instant familiarity]
  Bridge: IV – V – IV – V (C – D – C – D) [bouncing, staccato, building playful energy]
• Vocal: Male, relaxed, talk-singing, slightly deadpan on "I'm in cat jail" (the punchline lands flat = funnier), conversational warmth on verses, cheeky on bridge
• Instruments: Bright Rhodes piano, nylon fingerpicked guitar, soft bouncy bass (slightly funky), light programmed drums with rim clicks and hi-hats, handclaps (chorus), possible buried meow sample in final chorus (subtle, not gimmicky)
• Dynamics: Gentle groove (intro) → easy conversational (V1) → slight build (PC) → full bouncy pocket (C) → slightly pulled back (V2) → full again (C) → staccato energy (bridge) → biggest pocket (FC) → fading warmth (outro)
• Hook Type: Melodic chant hook ("La di da di da di da") + punchline payoff ("I'm in cat jail") — the la-di-da is the earworm, the reveal is the shareability
• Rhyme Scheme: V=AABB (couplets — conversational, bouncy), PC=AA (tight), C=AAA+punchline (chant + payoff), Bridge=AABB (storytelling)
• Emotional Arc: "This sounds romantic..." → "wait..." → "OH it's a CAT" → lean into the absurdity → pure joy of being trapped by something you love
