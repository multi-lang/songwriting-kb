## PHASE 1: ANALYSIS

### Step 1 — Song Thesis

**Song Thesis:** A lighthouse keeper finds sacred purpose in her daily solitude, believing the light she tends protects the world from the sea — unaware it protects the world from something IN the sea.

### Step 2 — Semantic Analysis

- **Subject:** A woman performing her dawn ritual of maintaining a lighthouse
- **Characters:** Maren, the keeper (alone)
- **Setting:** Remote lighthouse on a cliff, dawn breaking, calm sea below
- **Objects:** The lighthouse lamp, oil, glass lens, stone stairs, the sea, morning fog, concertina
- **Actions:** Climbing stairs, cleaning glass, lighting/trimming the wick, looking out at the empty sea, playing music to fill the silence

### Step 3 — Emotional Analysis

- **Central Affect:** Contented solitude threaded with unconscious loneliness — "purposeful isolation"
- **BRECVEMA Mechanisms:**
  1. **Rhythmic Entrainment** — the sea-wash and bodhran create a bodily settling, like a rocking chair
  2. **Episodic Memory** — the warmth and folk texture should evoke "morning routine" memories for the listener
  3. **Musical Expectancy** — the Mixolydian mode creates a bright-but-not-quite-major feeling (the b7 hints something isn't resolved)
- **Emotional Journey:** Starts at peace (pre-dawn quiet) → moves through purposeful activity (devotion) → arrives at pride tinged with unexamined loneliness

### Step 4 — Prosodic Planning

- **Target syllables:** 7-10 per line (92 BPM gives moderate space; not rushed)
- **Breath points:** End of every line (conversational phrasing); em-dash for mid-line pauses
- **Key vowels for sustain:** Open "ah" (D Mixolydian root), "oh" (on A notes), "ee" on high passages (E5 territory)
- **Default rhyme scheme:** ABCB (folk-ballad standard); slant rhymes preferred over forced perfect rhymes

### Step 5 — Narrative Arc

**Arc Type:** Isolation → devotion → pride (with unconscious irony)

- V1: The physical morning — waking, climbing, the routine
- V2: The emotional morning — why she stays, what the light means to her
- Bridge Turn: A quiet moment where she almost names the loneliness — but reframes it as purpose
- Final Chorus: Pride statement ("the light keeps ships from harm") — which the audience will later learn is not the full truth

**The Turn:** The bridge reveals she hasn't spoken to another person in weeks — but she tells herself this IS the purpose, not the cost.

### Step 6 — Voice/Accent

- **Register:** Female mezzo-soprano, staying in A3-C5 range (comfortable, warm, unforced)
- **Accent:** Slight coastal British (think Cornwall/Welsh coast) — not thick, just coloring
- **Emotional state at start:** Quiet contentment (pre-coffee calm)
- **Emotional state at end:** Quiet pride (she's done her duty, the light works, the world is safe)
- **Production:** Close-mic, dry, intimate. Like she's in the room with you.

### Step 7 — Genre Mapping

- **Primary genre (70%):** Acoustic folk (warm, handmade, conversational)
- **Texture genre (30%):** Sea-shanty inflection (rhythmic pulse from bodhran, community-song structure adapted for one voice)
- **Fabbri's 5 rules check:**
  - Formal: acoustic instrumentation, verse-chorus structure ✓
  - Semiotic: folk = authenticity, storytelling, connection to place ✓
  - Behavioral: intimate performance, close-mic'd ✓
  - Social/ideological: working-class, coastal, duty ✓
  - Juridical/commercial: accessible, singable ✓
- **Instruments that "belong":** Concertina, bodhran, acoustic guitar, fiddle (restrained), vocal

### Step 8 — Arrangement Design

- **Growth pattern:** Additive bloom — starts with sea-wash only, adds concertina, adds voice, adds bodhran, adds guitar. Builds gently.
- **Negative space:** Intro (sea-wash alone), bridge (strip to voice + concertina only)
- **Contrast pairs:** Silence/warmth, solitude/purpose, sea/stone
- **Hook placement:** Hook line ("the light keeps ships from harm") arrives in chorus. With intro ~12s and V1 starting at ~12s, chorus hits by ~45s. Pre-chorus is short (2 lines) to ensure hook arrives within 15s of first vocal if we count from verse start. Actually — restructure: very short intro (4-8s of sea-wash), voice enters immediately. Hook must land by ~15s of first vocal. Solution: use a brief verse (4 lines) before a short pre-chorus (2 lines) into chorus. At 92 BPM (~1.5s/line with breath), V1 = ~8s, pre-chorus = ~4s. Hook lands at ~12-13s of first vocal. ✓

### Step 9 — Production & Commercial

- **Key:** D Mixolydian — reasoning: the album's "home" key, represents the keeper's world. The b7 (C natural) gives warmth without full major brightness — something is open-ended, unresolved.
- **BPM:** 92 — reasoning: matches T8 (structural callback/heartbeat). Walking-pace, unhurried, ritualistic. Not slow enough for ballad, not fast enough for energy.
- **Target audience:** Concept-album listeners, folk-horror fans, Suno AI enthusiasts
- **Target length:** 210-240 seconds (3:30-4:00) — full album opener, needs to establish world
- **Platform fit:** Suno AI generation → streaming. Album-first listening.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Key
**D Mixolydian** — The album's home key. Represents the keeper's world. The raised 4th-scale degree (b7 = C natural instead of C#) creates warmth without full major resolution — something remains open, unsettled beneath the contentment. This is the ONLY track (with T8) allowed this key.

### Tempo
**92 BPM** — Walking pace, ritualistic. Not slow enough for a ballad (which would suggest sadness), not fast enough for energy (which would break the solitude). Matches T8 exactly — the heartbeat that bookends the album.

### Time Signature
**4/4** — Steady, predictable, like a routine performed for sixty years. The bodhran and sea-wash reinforce the regularity. No metric surprises — this is safety.

### Chord Progression

**Verse:**
Nashville: I - bVII - IV - I
Actual: D - C - G - D
(The Mixolydian signature: I-bVII is the sound of "almost home." Resolves to I but the C keeps pulling.)

**Pre-Chorus:**
Nashville: IV - V - bVII
Actual: G - A - C
(Rising tension — the A major provides a brief leading-tone feel before the C pulls it back Mixolydian.)

**Chorus:**
Nashville: I - IV - bVII - I
Actual: D - G - C - D
(Same DNA as verse but wider voicing. The hook sits on the I chord — maximum stability for a declarative statement.)

**Bridge:**
Nashville: bVII - IV - I - V
Actual: C - G - D - A
(Starts AWAY from home on bVII — she's admitting something uncomfortable. Returns to I mid-phrase. The A at the end creates yearning back to D for the final chorus.)

### Vocal Description
Female mezzo-soprano (A3-C5 working range). Warm, conversational, slight coastal rasp. Close-mic'd, dry (minimal reverb). No harmony — solo voice only. She sounds like she's singing to fill a room, not to perform. Think: Shirley Collins meets Lisa Hannigan in texture.

### Instruments (by prominence)
1. **Voice** — lead, center, close-mic, dry
2. **Concertina** — co-lead in intro/outro, support in verses, establishes Keeper's Melody motif
3. **Bodhran** — rhythmic pulse from V1 onward, gentle, steady (sea-rhythm parallel)
4. **Acoustic guitar** — fingerpicked arpeggios, enters in chorus, adds warmth/fullness
5. **Sea-wash field recording** — constant, low-mix (treat as percussion/texture), real recorded waves
