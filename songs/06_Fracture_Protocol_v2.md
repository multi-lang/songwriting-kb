# Fracture Protocol — Polished (Album Track 6)

> Structure tightening. This song is ambitious and largely effective — it's the most
> chaotic track in the album (intentionally). Issues: V1 had some lines that told rather
> than showed, the Breakdown section repeated material from the chorus (redundant),
> and a few parenthetical whispers were too explanatory. Sharpened imagery,
> trimmed redundancy, made the body horror more visceral. The chaos is INTENTIONAL
> but it needs to be controlled chaos, not scattered chaos.

---

## STYLE PROMPT:
Dark cinematic industrial, glitchy cyber-noir psychosis, cold fractured baritone A2–E4 with heavy digital degradation, key F minor, 4/4, 84 BPM, massive distorted drums, crushing sub bass, fragmented piano, cold dissonant synths, glitch textures, low eerie strings, corrupted signal ambience, haunting atmosphere

[Exclusions: ‑bright synths, ‑warm production, ‑EDM drops, ‑pop vocals, ‑upbeat energy, ‑trap drums, ‑cheerful melodies, ‑heroic orchestration, ‑vocal hums, ‑beatboxing, ‑clean polished mix, ‑triumphant endings]

## PRODUCTION NOTES:

• Key: F minor — dissonant, uncomfortable, rarely used (unfamiliar = disorienting), dark
• Tempo: 84 BPM (heartbeat pace — but irregular, glitching, as if the pulse itself is fracturing)
• Time Signature: 4/4 (but should feel like it's losing time — production tricks: stutters, dropped beats)
• Chord Progression:
  Verse: i – ii° – iv – VII (Fm – Gdim – Bbm – Eb) [dissonant, unstable, diminished = wrongness]
  Pre-Chorus: iv – VII – i – i (Bbm – Eb – Fm – Fm) [trying to resolve, failing]
  Chorus: i – VI – VII – v (Fm – Db – Eb – Cm) [wide but unstable, no resolution]
  Bridge: i chromatic descent (Fm – E – Eb – D) [reality sliding]
  Final Chorus: i – VII – VI – VII (Fm – Eb – Db – Eb) [circular, no escape, no landing]
  Outro: i fading to silence (Fm dissolving into cave ambience)
• Vocal: Male baritone (A2–E4), dissociated whisper → panicked fragments → trapped scream → terrified arrival
• Instruments: Fragmented piano, massive distorted drums, crushing sub bass, cold dissonant synths, glitch textures constantly, low eerie strings, distant war horns (final chorus), wet cave reverb (outro), mechanical heartbeat (throughout — failing in outro)
• Dynamics: Dissociated whisper (intro) → unstable fragments (V1) → building dread (PC) → MASSIVE chaotic overload (C) → sudden silence (post-C) → body horror intimacy (V2) → whispered identity war (bridge) → full chaotic force (FC) → collapse into cave silence (outro)
• Hook Type: System command as hook — "Fracture Protocol" repeated as both threat and reality. Secondary: "I built the world inside the vault / Now it's pulling pieces out of me"
• Rhyme Scheme: V=ABCB loose (fragmented, dissociated — rhyme would feel too ordered for this state), C=Free, Bridge=call-and-response (identity arguing with itself)
• Emotional Arc: Confusion → splitting → panic → body horror → identity loss → war between selves → full fracture → terrified arrival in the unknown

## CHANGES FROM ORIGINAL:

1. **V1 line 3:** "My reflection won't look at me / It knows things I've tried to hide" → "My reflection blinked before I did / It knows things I've tried to hide" (more specific horror — the reflection MOVED FIRST, implying separate agency)
2. **V1 line 5:** "The glass is pulsing like it's alive" → "The glass is pulsing like a living thing" (avoids the simile-cliché "like it's alive" — "living thing" is stranger, more unsettling)
3. **V1 line 7:** "Lights bend and twist into shapes / Like veins under someone else's skin" → "Lights bend into shapes like veins / Under someone else's skin" (tightened rhythm — line break creates the pause)
4. **Pre-Chorus:** "I'm on both sides / (Am I on both sides…)" → "I'm standing on both sides / (am I on both sides)" (added "standing" — physical, grounded, makes the impossibility more visceral)
5. **V2 line 3-4:** Removed "(I had hands… soft hands…)" linebreak, tightened to single parenthetical for rhythm
6. **V2 line 7:** "Something small and fast inside my chest / (I had a life…)" → "Something small and fast inside my chest / A second heartbeat — or a first" (much stronger — IS this heartbeat new or was the old one the imposter? Then the whispered "(I had a life)" lands harder after)
7. **Removed entire Breakdown section** — it repeated "I was a body / I was a name / I was a voice inside a room" and the chorus refrain. This material was redundant (the Final Chorus already hits these themes). Removing it tightens the song by ~20 lines and makes the jump from Bridge identity-war directly into Final Chorus more impactful.
8. **Bridge tightened** — removed "I know this place" setup line, goes straight into the argument. Faster, more disorienting.
9. **Added format standardization** — [Title:], [Exclusions:], direction blocks at top
