# Fractured Plains — Album Track 10 (v3)

> Song Thesis: *You learn what you're made of when there's no one to perform for —
> just miles of broken earth and the decision to keep walking.*

---

## STYLE PROMPT:
Epic cinematic dark folk rock, atmospheric journey song, male baritone A2-E4, key D minor, 4/4, 88 BPM, driving acoustic guitar foundation, cinematic percussion building, low cello drone, wide atmospheric pads, distant war drums on horizon, desert wind textures, Nordic folk undertones, progressive build from sparse to massive, landscape as character

[Exclusions: ‑bright pop, ‑trap drums, ‑EDM drops, ‑beatboxing, ‑vocal hums, ‑cheerful melodies, ‑autotune, ‑industrial textures, ‑glitch effects, ‑electronic percussion, ‑hip hop rhythms]

---

## LYRICS:

[Title: Fractured Plains]

[Production Direction:
The JOURNEY song — weeks of walking compressed into 4 minutes. Solo fingerpicked acoustic guitar + wind opening. Build GRADUALLY: cello enters V2, percussion as distant thuds, full drums at final chorus. Never loses acoustic guitar foundation — it's his heartbeat/footsteps. Bridge strips to water + gentle guitar. Final chorus: full cinematic power. Target 4:15.]

[Vocal Direction:
Male baritone — weathered, road-worn storytelling. Verses conversational. Pre-chorus builds with determination. Chorus: almost anthemic but lonely. Bridge: soft, grateful. Final chorus: full power, earned — the city appearing.]

[Intro - Emergence]
[Fingerpicked acoustic guitar] [Wind] [Bright open reverb]

The sun hits like a fist after weeks of dark
The ground is cracked — the sky is wrong
But there's a sky — and that's enough to start

[Verse 1 - The Desolate Plain]
[Fingerpicked acoustic] [Wind] [Close-mic vocal]

The cave mouth spat me on to broken ground
A landscape torn like parchment left to burn
The air tastes sharp — like ozone and old dust
The shard is pulling east — so east I turn

No roads — no signs — just fractures in the earth
And heat that presses down like something grieving
I count my steps to keep my sanity
Two thousand gone before I stop believing

[Pre-Chorus - Determination]
[Low cello drone enters] [Guitar strumming drives]

The shard says east — my legs say stop
My lungs say I was never built for this

But I keep walking
Because stopping means the man I was — is gone

[Chorus - The Long Walk]
[Drums enter — distant, marching] [Cello swells] [Guitar drives] [Wide pad]

Fractured plains beneath my feet
Miles of silence — miles of heat
Nothing living wants me here
But the shard keeps pulling — and I follow

Fractured plains — I walk alone
Through a world of shattered stone
Every step's a choice I make
To keep becoming — or to break

[Verse 2 - Riftborn Territory]
[Drums steady] [Cello sustains] [Guitar darker]

The wolves came on the third day — scorched-earth fur
Eyes that burned with something worse than hunger
I used the canyon walls — I used the dark
Escaped their jaws by less than half a shadow

The ruins came next — obsidian and bone
Glyphs that matched the ones beneath my skin
They told of people who had mastered this
And paid the price for what they tried to win

[Pre-Chorus 2 - Learning]
[Percussion fuller] [Strings build]

I'm learning how to read this land
Its rhythms and its rage
Which shadows hold a cage

I'm not surviving anymore
I'm starting to belong

[Bridge - The Oasis]
[Everything drops] [Water sounds] [Soft acoustic only] [Vocal tender]

A valley hidden from the heat
A stream so clear it hurt to look
I drank until my hands stopped shaking
And washed the miles from off my skin

The shard pulsed gently — not demanding
For one night in this shattered world
I let myself be small — and grateful
And something close to still

[Final Chorus - Dawnwatch Appears]
[Full cinematic drums] [Cello + strings wide] [Guitar powerful] [Distant choir pad]

Fractured plains beneath my feet
Weeks of silence — weeks of heat
Mountains rising — mist ahead
The shard is burning brighter than it ever has

And there — beyond the final ridge
A city made of light

I kept walking
And the walking made me real

[Outro - Descent]
[Guitar gentles] [Strings sustain] [Shard pulse steady]

The descent begins
The city hums beneath me
And I begin again

## PRODUCTION NOTES:

• Key: D minor — cinematic, wide, cello-friendly, carries journey weight
• Tempo: 88 BPM (walking pace — steady, relentless)
• Time Signature: 4/4
• Chord Progression:
  Verse: i – VII – VI – III (Dm – C – Bb – F) [wide, cinematic landscape]
  Pre-Chorus: iv – i – VII – VII (Gm – Dm – C – C) [building, pushing forward]
  Chorus: i – VII – VI – VII (Dm – C – Bb – C) [driving, marching, never resolves = journey not over]
  Bridge: VI – III – VII – i (Bb – F – C – Dm) [gentler, oasis = brief resolution]
  Final Chorus: VI – VII – III – i (Bb – C – F – Dm) [wider reach, Dawnwatch = hope, returns Dm = continues]
• Vocal: Male baritone (A2–E4), road-worn → determined → tender → full epic power
• Instruments: Fingerpicked acoustic (constant), cello drone, cinematic percussion, wide pads, wind textures, water (bridge), distant choir pad (FC)
• Dynamics: Solo acoustic (intro) → storytelling (V1) → cello + drive (PC) → marching drums (C) → darker intensity (V2) → full (PC2) → stripped water + guitar (bridge) → FULL cinematic (FC) → gentle (outro)
• Hook Type: "Fractured plains beneath my feet" (title) + "the walking made me real" (payoff)
• Rhyme Scheme: V=ABCB, PC=Free (tight), C=AABB (marching), Bridge=ABCB
• Emotional Arc: Emergence → desolation → survival → learning → one night of peace → city appears → arrival
