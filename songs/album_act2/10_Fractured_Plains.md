# Fractured Plains — Album Track 10

> Rikan emerges from the Whispering Caves into a desolate, shattered landscape.
> The journey to Dawnwatch takes weeks — across cracked earth, through Riftborn
> wolf territory, past ancient ruins covered in glyphs, through corrupted forests.
> He's alone the entire time. This song is about epic solitude — the long walk
> through a hostile world with nothing but purpose and pain for company.
> The landscape IS the character here. Eryndor is vast, broken, beautiful, and deadly.
>
> Song Thesis: *You learn what you're made of when there's no one to perform for —
> just miles of broken earth and the decision to keep walking.*

---

## STYLE PROMPT:
Epic cinematic dark folk rock, atmospheric journey song, male baritone A2-E4, key D minor, 4/4, 88 BPM, driving acoustic guitar foundation, cinematic percussion building through song, low cello drone, wide atmospheric pads, distant war drums on horizon, desert wind textures, epic scale with intimate vocal delivery, Nordic folk undertones, progressive build from sparse to massive, landscape as character

[Exclusions: ‑bright pop, ‑trap drums, ‑EDM drops, ‑beatboxing, ‑vocal hums, ‑cheerful melodies, ‑autotune, ‑industrial textures, ‑glitch effects, ‑electronic percussion, ‑hip hop rhythms, ‑clean polished production]

## LYRICS:

[Title: Fractured Plains]

[Production Direction:
This is the JOURNEY song — it needs to feel like weeks of walking compressed into 4 minutes. Start with a single acoustic guitar (fingerpicked, open tuning) and wind texture — the emergence from the caves into blinding sun. Build GRADUALLY: cello drone enters verse 2, percussion begins as distant thuds (like footsteps or a war drum on the horizon), full cinematic drums arrive in the final chorus. The song should feel like a landscape expanding — intimate and small at first, then vast and overwhelming. The oasis moment (bridge) strips back to gentle water sounds and relief before the final push. Never loses the acoustic guitar foundation — it's his heartbeat, his footsteps, his constant.]

[Vocal Direction:
Male baritone — weathered, road-worn, storytelling delivery. Verses are conversational — a man narrating his own survival to stay sane. Pre-choruses build with determination. Chorus opens into something bigger — almost anthemic but lonely, like shouting into empty space. Bridge is soft, grateful, a moment of peace. Final chorus: full power, earned, the city appearing on the horizon.]

The sun hits like a fist

The cave mouth spat me out on broken ground

No roads — no signs — just fractures in the earth

I count my steps to keep my sanity
Two thousand gone before I stop believing

That someone's going to wake me from this
That somewhere there's a screen — a desk — a life
But the claws that grip the rock are mine

## PRODUCTION NOTES:

• **Title:** Fractured Plains
• **Song Thesis:** *You learn what you're made of when there's no one to perform for — just miles of broken earth and the decision to keep walking.*
• **Key:** D minor — cinematic, wide, cello-friendly, carries weight and journey, darker than Em but more forward-moving
• **Tempo:** 88 BPM (walking pace — steady, relentless, the tempo of putting one foot in front of the other for weeks)
• **Time Signature:** 4/4
• **Chord Progression:**
  - Verse: i – VII – VI – III (Dm – C – Bb – F) [wide, open, cinematic landscape — the "journey" progression]
  - Pre-Chorus: iv – i – VII – VII (Gm – Dm – C – C) [building, unresolved, pushing forward]
  - Chorus: i – VII – VI – VII (Dm – C – Bb – C) [driving, marching, anthemic-minor — never resolves to major = journey not over]
  - Bridge: VI – III – VII – i (Bb – F – C – Dm) [gentler, more open — the oasis = brief resolution before return]
  - Final Chorus: VI – VII – III – i (Bb – C – F – Dm) [wider — reaches toward F major (relative) = Dawnwatch appearing = hope, but returns to Dm = journey continues]
• **Vocal:** Male baritone (A2–E4), road-worn storytelling → building determination → tender oasis relief → full epic power at Dawnwatch reveal
• **Instruments:** Fingerpicked acoustic guitar (constant — the heartbeat/footstep of the song), low cello drone (enters V2, builds), cinematic percussion (distant march → full kit by FC), wide atmospheric pads, distant war drums (horizon texture), wind textures, gentle water (bridge only), distant choir pad (FC only)
• **Dynamics:** Solo acoustic + wind (intro) → storytelling intimacy (V1) → cello + driving strum (PC) → drums enter, marching (C) → darker intensity (V2) → building fullness (PC2) → total strip to water + gentle guitar (bridge) → FULL cinematic power (FC) → gentle resolution (outro)
• **Hook Type:** Title hook — "Fractured plains beneath my feet" as recurring opening line of chorus + "I kept walking / And the walking made me real" as emotional payoff hook
• **Rhyme Scheme:** V=ABCB (folk storytelling — conversational), PC=ABCB (building), C=AABB (driving couplets — marching rhythm matches walking), Bridge=Free (tender, prose-like)
• **Emotional Arc:** Blinding emergence → determination through desolation → survival against wildlife → learning the world → one night of peace → the city appears → arrival — the journey MADE him, not just transported him
• **Continuity:** Opens where Track 9 ended (climbing out). The acoustic guitar foundation marks the SONIC SHIFT of Act 2 — we've left the industrial/electronic world of Act 1 behind. The organic instruments (acoustic, cello, live drums) now dominate. The shard pulse motif continues. Ends with descent into Dawnwatch = Track 11's setting.
• **Nordic Folk Influence:** The driving acoustic pattern + cello + marching percussion draws from the Nordic folk metal tradition — connects to the fantasy world's cultural DNA without being explicitly "Viking."
