# The Sword Remembers — Album Track 15 (v3)

> Song Thesis: *Grief isn't the weight of someone's absence — it's the weight of
> everything they gave you that you now have to carry alone.*

---

## STYLE PROMPT:
Cinematic grief ballad, orchestral folk rock, deeply emotional, male baritone A2-E4, key G minor, 4/4, 68 BPM, solo piano opening, strings building to full orchestra, acoustic guitar midway, gentle war drums final chorus only, wind through ruins, eulogy energy, warmth beneath sorrow

[Exclusions: ‑bright pop, ‑trap drums, ‑EDM drops, ‑beatboxing, ‑vocal hums, ‑cheerful melodies, ‑autotune, ‑industrial textures, ‑glitch effects, ‑aggressive guitar, ‑fast tempo]

---

## LYRICS:

[Title: The Sword Remembers]

[Production Direction:
The grief song. Solo piano playing Alaric's theme (unique motif — introduced and retired here). Let piano carry V1 ALONE. Strings enter pre-chorus. Chorus: full but RESTRAINED. Acoustic guitar enters V2 (warmth of memory). Bridge: Alaric's spoken voice from Track 11 returns as ghost. Final chorus: gentle war drums + faint choir. End on piano theme alone. Target 4:30. This should make people cry.]

[Vocal Direction:
Male baritone — raw, exposed, close-mic. Voice of grief that hasn't found its shape. Verses barely above speaking. Pre-chorus: cracks. Chorus: full but RESTRAINED — power through control. Bridge: quiet, speaking TO Alaric. Final chorus: grief becoming purpose. Never belts. Power is in restraint.]

[Intro - The Ruins]
[Wind through broken stone] [Solo piano — Alaric's theme]

The wind moves through what's left of him
Even in ruin — he refuses to fall

[Verse 1 - The Statue]
[Solo piano] [Close-mic] [Wind]

I trace my fingers on the broken stone
His face half-gone — but I know every line
The jaw that set before he spoke
The eyes that held a room in place

I remember mornings in the training yard
His voice cutting through fog like certainty
"Again" — he'd say — when everything in me
Wanted to collapse — "Again — but better"

[Pre-Chorus - The Crack]
[Strings enter — aching] [Piano deepens]

I didn't get to say goodbye
The fire took that too
I have his lessons but I don't have him
And lessons change when the teacher's gone

[Chorus - The Sword]
[Full strings — aching, wide] [Piano + acoustic guitar]

The sword remembers what the hand forgets
The stance holds steady when the heart gives way
He taught me how to fight — but more than that
He taught me what was worth the fight

The sword remembers every morning drill
The muscle memory of a man who cared
His voice lives in the way I hold my ground
His faith — in every battle I've prepared

The sword remembers
Even when the hand that forged it — is gone

[Verse 2 - Quiet Moments]
[Acoustic guitar — warm fingerpick] [Piano continues] [Strings gentle]

I remember evenings by the fire
His stories longer than the candles burned
He told me once — the night before the breach
"Trust the people standing next to you"

I didn't understand then — I was focused
On the training — on the Mark — the fight
But standing here with nothing left of him
I understand — the trust was his last gift

He trusted me before I earned it
And that — that was the blade he really left

[Pre-Chorus 2]
[Strings swell] [Guitar arpeggios]

What do you do with a dead man's faith
When you're not sure you've grown into its shape
What do you do with lessons from a ghost
When every one still sounds like hope

[Bridge - His Voice Returns]
[Everything drops — wind + single piano note]

[Spoken — Alaric, ghostly, echoing]
"The sword is but an extension of the heart, Rikan"

[Whispered — speaking to Alaric]
I hear you — in the stone — in the wind
You're not here but you're not gone
You're in my spine — in my stance
In the way I refuse to kneel

I will be the thing you saw in me
Before I could see it myself

[Final Chorus - Legacy]
[Strings warm] [Guitar + piano] [Gentle war drums] [Faint choir]

The sword remembers what the hand forgets
The heart holds steady when the world gives way
He taught me how to stand — and more than that
He taught me what to stand for — every day

The sword remembers — and so do I
Not just the combat — but the quiet parts
The meals — the stories — the belief he carried
Like a lantern through the dark

The sword remembers
Not for revenge — not for glory
But because he asked me to

[Outro - The Theme Fades]
[Piano alone — Alaric's theme, slower] [Wind calming] [Single bird]

Goodbye old man
I'll make it count

## PRODUCTION NOTES:

• Key: G minor — deep grief (Mozart's requiem key), warm enough for tenderness
• Tempo: 68 BPM (dirge pace — slowest on album)
• Time Signature: 4/4
• Chord Progression:
  Verse: i – VI – III – VII (Gm – Eb – Bb – F) [spacious, room for grief]
  Pre-Chorus: iv – i – VII – VI (Cm – Gm – F – Eb) [descending into ache]
  Chorus: VI – III – VII – i (Eb – Bb – F – Gm) [reaching toward warmth, returning to absence]
  Bridge: i (Gm sustained → single note)
  Final Chorus: VI – VII – III – i (Eb – F – Bb – Gm) [wider reach, legacy IS warmth]
• Vocal: Male baritone (A2–D4), raw grief → cracking → controlled ache → speaking to dead → resolved
• Instruments: Solo piano (Alaric's unique theme), strings, acoustic guitar (V2+), gentle war drums (FC only), wind, faint choir (FC), Alaric's spoken voice (bridge ghost)
• Dynamics: Wind + piano (intro) → piano + voice (V1) → strings enter (PC) → full aching (C) → warmer guitar (V2) → stripped (bridge) → full warm + drums (FC) → piano alone (outro)
• Hook Type: "The sword remembers what the hand forgets" (metaphor hook) + Alaric quote callback
• Rhyme Scheme: V=ABCB, PC=AABB, C=ABAB, Bridge=Free
• Emotional Arc: Standing in ruins → remembering → grief → quiet moments → hearing his voice → purpose
