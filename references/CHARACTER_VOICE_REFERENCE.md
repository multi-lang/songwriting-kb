# Character Voice Design Reference

> Complete system for designing character-driven vocal performances including accent,
> dialect, phonetics, cultural instrumentation, and mode selection.

---

## CHARACTER VOICE DESIGN TEMPLATE

```
CHARACTER VOICE:
• Name/Identity: [who is speaking]
• Accent base: [real-world phonetic basis]
• Vowel character: [open/closed, long/short, warm/cold]
• Consonant character: [rolled R, glottal stops, soft, hard, etc.]
• Rhythmic tendency: [behind beat, on beat, ahead, flowing]
• Register: [bass, baritone, tenor, alto, soprano]
• Texture: [gravelly, smooth, breathy, raspy, clear]
• Emotional subtext: [what does the voice FEEL like]
• Dialect words: [specific vocabulary reinforcing accent]
• Instruments that match: [cultural instrumentation]
• Production space: [where does this voice live]
• Mode/scale: [harmonic language matching culture]
```

## ACCENT REFERENCE — REAL WORLD

| Accent | Key Features | Vowels | Consonants | Instruments | Genre |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Southern US** | Drawl, diphthongs wide, dropped g's | Open, warm, long | Soft, dropped | Acoustic, pedal steel, fiddle | Country, Southern rock |
| **Irish** | Soft T's, lilting cadence, musical | Round, flowing | Soft, melodic | Fiddle, tin whistle, bodhrán | Celtic folk, Irish punk |
| **Scottish** | Rolled R's, guttural, burr | Round, resonant, deep | Rolled R, guttural | Bagpipes, fiddle, clàrsach | Celtic rock, folk |
| **Nordic** | Pitch accent, clear vowels, melodic | Clear, bright, tonal | Soft, precise | Nyckelharpa, hardingfele, chant | Nordic folk, Viking metal |
| **Cockney** | Glottal stops, H-dropping | Short, punchy | Dropped, glottal | — | Punk, grime, ska |
| **Appalachian** | Mountain twang, nasal, archaic | High nasal, tight | Clipped | Banjo, dulcimer, fiddle | Bluegrass, folk |

## VOWEL PLACEMENT & SUSTAINED NOTES

| Accent | Best Sustained Vowels | Avoid Sustaining |
|---|---|---|
| Southern US | "ah", "ay", "oh" | Short "i" |
| Irish | "oh", "ah", "ee" — all musical | — |
| Scottish | "ah", "oh", rolled "r" can sustain | Short vowels |
| Nordic | Clear "a", "e", "o" | Nasal vowels |
| Elvish | All sustain beautifully | — |
| Dwarvish | Short vowels — DON'T sustain | Long flowing vowels |

## RHYTHMIC PHRASING BY ACCENT

| Accent | Phrasing | Beat Relationship |
|---|---|---|
| Southern | Long and lazy — words stretch | Behind the beat |
| Irish | Lilting — words dance | On or slightly ahead |
| Scottish | Deliberate — words land with weight | On the beat (grounded) |
| Nordic | Steady, measured, ritual | On the beat (inevitable) |
| Dwarvish | Percussive — words are HITS | On the beat (hammering) |
| Elvish | Flowing — across barlines | Free/rubato |

## DIALECT WORDS FOR LYRIC REINFORCEMENT

| Accent | Words to Use | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Southern | ain't, gonna, y'all, reckon | Grounds the vocal |
| Irish | aye, lass, grand, fierce | Triggers the lilt |
| Scottish | ye, yer, aye, wee, braw, cannae | Forces rolled R's |
| Appalachian | git, cain't, holler, creek (crik) | Mountain placement |
| Dwarvish-inspired | stone, forge, iron, ale, oath, clan | Grounding words |
| Nordic-inspired | fate, rune, sea, storm, oath, blood | Ancient weight |
