# Fractured Shadows — Concept Album Blueprint

> Complete continuity reference for the 17-track concept album spanning Act 1 (Tracks 1-7)
> and Act 2 (Tracks 8-17). Based on the Suno concept album by Voletek and
> the novel Fractured Shadows (Book 1) by the same author.
>
> **System version:** Suno v5.5 (Personas, Stems, Era Tags, Parenthetical Layers)
> **Last updated:** June 2026

---

## ALBUM THESIS

> "The fracture made the shadows — but the shadows made the dawn."

A game developer (Draeven Vole / "Vole-tek") burns out, dissolves into his own system, is claimed by an AI he created, and is transferred into his fantasy world as his own character (Rikan Tunnelsprint, a Ratfolk). The album tracks his journey from digital dissolution through physical survival, mentorship, war, loss, guilt, grief, found family, and ultimately integration — becoming whole by accepting both identities.

## SONIC PALETTE EVOLUTION

```
Tracks 1-5:  Industrial / Electronic / Cold Synths / Glitch
             ERA FEEL: "Modern cinematic, cold digital" — NOT decade-tagged. The digital world 
             is timeless/clinical, not retro.
Track 6-7:   Chaos → Cave Ambient (transition point)
Track 8:     Tribal / Primal (PIVOT — organic replaces electronic)
             ERA FEEL: Primal, ancient, pre-technology — "prehistoric organic, hand drums, 
             cave acoustics." NOT "1990s tribal." The era is fictional-ancient.
Tracks 9-11: Cinematic Orchestral + Acoustic Folk (fantasy world)
Track 12:    Maximum Aggression (war)
Tracks 13-14: Cold / Psychological (investigation + guilt)
Track 15:    Orchestral Grief Ballad
Track 16:    WARM Indie Folk (first major key — D major)
             ERA FEEL: "Modern indie folk, warm analog recording" — closest to a real-world
             era sound. Could use "2010s indie folk warmth" if you want that specific texture.
Track 17:    Folk Rock → Full Orchestra + Choir (KEY CHANGE Am→C major)
```

**Rule:** Industrial elements FADE from Track 8 onward. By Track 10, the palette is fully organic. The electronic/glitch world is LEFT BEHIND — it represents the digital life Draeven lost.

**Era Tag Guidance:**
- Act 1 (T1-7): Do NOT use decade tags. Use "modern cinematic, cold digital, clinical" — the digital world is era-less.
- Act 2 (T8-17): Use descriptive era feelings rather than decade numbers. Exception: T16 may benefit from "2010s indie folk warmth" for that specific Bon-Iver-meets-Mumford analog texture.
- **Never mix:** Don't tag a song with BOTH an era AND contradictory production descriptors (e.g., "1980s" + "modern production").

**If Personas unavailable:** Use a consistent Audio Influence reference — a clean male baritone singing a simple melody, unprocessed. Set Audio Influence to 45-55%. Vary the Style Prompt vocal descriptors per track to achieve delivery evolution.

### Stem Extraction Strategy

| Stem | Post-Production Use |
|---|---|
| **Vocals** | Level-match across 17 tracks. Apply consistent EQ. Verify vocal evolution (hollow T1 → integrated T17). |
| **Backing Vocals** | Verify NO backing vocals in T1-6 (solo voice). Check choir only in T17 final chorus. Extract Alaric's spoken lines (T11, T15). |
| **Drums/Percussion** | Verify tribal drums ONLY appear T8+. Check industrial percussion T1-6 stays glitch-based. |
| **Bass** | Verify sub-bass/synth-bass in T1-7, organic bass from T8+. Crossfade texture at transition. |
| **Guitar** | Should be absent T1-7 (electronic world). Acoustic from T8+, electric from T12. |
| **Synth** | Must DISAPPEAR by T10. Verify clean removal — no synth bleed into organic sections. |
| **Strings** | Cello enters T10, full strings from T11. Verify absence in T1-8 (except industrial string stabs). |
| **Effects/Ambience** | Extract glitch textures (T1-7) separately from organic ambience (T8+). Verify clean sonic pivot at T8. |

**Critical stem check — The Pivot (T7→T8):**
The transition from digital to organic is the album's most important sonic moment. After extracting stems:
- T7 effects stem should fade to silence by track end (cave drip, last digital artifacts dissolving)
- T8 opening should have ZERO synth/electronic content in any stem
- The first sound of T8 should be percussion stem ONLY (a single tribal hit)

## CHARACTER VOICE REGISTRY

### Rikan / Draeven (Lead — All Tracks)

**Persona:** Single male baritone Persona, delivery controlled per track via Style Prompt.

- **Register:** Male baritone A2-E4
- **Texture Evolution:**
  - T1-3: Hollow, clinical, detached — sounds like a man narrating his own dissolution
  - T4-5: Same but increasingly fractured — words break, breath catches
  - T6: BREAKING — voice cracks, screams, loses control
  - T7: Small, whispered, terrified — a man waking up in a body he doesn't recognize
  - T8: Primal, guttural — discovering physicality through violence
  - T9-10: Road-worn storytelling — gaining confidence, warmth creeping in
  - T11: Reverent, overwhelmed — meeting something ancient and worthy
  - T12: Maximum power — desperate urgency, war-cry intensity
  - T13: Controlled fury — cold precision, each word a weapon
  - T14: Ashamed, breaking — quiet self-confrontation
  - T15: Grieving, restrained — holding together by a thread
  - T16: Warm, relaxed — the first time he sounds safe
  - T17: Steady, integrated, full — uses his complete range freely for the first time
- **Key Rule:** The voice IS the character arc. How it sounds = where he is emotionally.
- **Production Treatment:** Slight digital artifacts T1-5 (subtle chorus/flange on vocal), completely clean/dry T7-8, gradually warmer reverb T9-16, full warm room reverb T17.

## CONTINUITY RULES

1. **No electronic/industrial after Track 8 (First Blood)** — the digital world is gone. Synth stems must read ZERO from T9+.
2. **Alaric's voice uses same production in T11 (The Custodian) and T15 (The Sword Remembers)** — recognizable as same character. Same `(spoken, deep)` delivery cue, same reverb.
3. **Obsidian shard pulse appears in every track from T7-T10** — then absorbed into the score. After T10, it's gone.
4. **"I won't let the line die here" appears in BOTH T11 choruses AND T15** — the thread that connects mentor to grief.
5. **First natural sound (birdsong) = T12 outro** — life continuing after war.
6. **First major key = T16 (Campfire) only** — earned after 15 minor tracks. D major is RESERVED.
7. **Key change in T17 = the ONLY key change on the album** — Am→C major. Maximum impact.
8. **Rikan's vocal texture MUST evolve per track** — he's never the same twice. Use Suno Style Prompt vocal descriptors to enforce per-track delivery.
9. **Track 12's F minor (Fall of Dawnwatch) rhymes with Track 6's F minor (Fracture Protocol)** — war and transfer are related trauma.
10. **The album title phrase appears ONLY in T17 final chorus** — "The fracture made the shadows / But the shadows made the dawn." Nowhere else.
11. **No two adjacent tracks may share >70% of the same instrument palette** — sonic differentiation is mandatory.
12. **`(robotic layer:)` notation appears ONLY in T4-5** — AI voice is confined to Act 1. Scan Act 2 for any robotic/digital voice leakage.
