# Song Critique Reference — Professional Evaluation Framework

> A multi-layered critique system combining Nashville craft assessment, academic musicology
> (Moore, Tagg, Lacasse, Moylan), A&R industry evaluation, and concept album narrative analysis.
> Used by the Critic agent for consistent, actionable, academically-grounded assessment.
>
> **Last updated:** June 2026

---

## OVERVIEW — THREE-LAYER CRITIQUE MODEL

```
Layer 1: CORE 12 CATEGORIES (Craft)        — "Is it well-made?"
Layer 2: ADVANCED 5 ASSESSMENTS (Depth)     — "Is it meaningful and rewarding?"
Layer 3: ALBUM-CONTEXT MODULE (Continuity)  — "Does it serve its role?" (concept albums only)
+ CALIBRATION & TECHNICAL AUDIT             — "Are the systems working correctly?"
```

**Which layers to use:**

| Scenario | Layers |
|---|---|
| Quick feedback | Layer 1 only |
| Full pipeline (SOP 07 Stage 2) | Layers 1 + 2 |
| Concept album track | Layers 1 + 2 + 3 + Calibration |
| Standalone single | Layers 1 + 2 + Calibration |
| Re-critique after revision | Layer 1 + delta from previous |

## LAYER 1: THE 12-CATEGORY SCORING RUBRIC (1-10 each)

### 1. HOOK (Memorable? Singable? Arrives within 7-15s?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Instantly memorable, arrives early, works a cappella, textable, melodically distinct |
| 7-8 | Strong phrase, good repetition, slightly late arrival or relies on context |
| 5-6 | Functional but not sticky, arrives too late, or too generic |
| 3-4 | Weak — forgettable, buried, or unclear what the hook IS |
| 1-2 | No identifiable hook |

**Hook timing verification:** At the song's BPM, count ~2-3 seconds per sung line with breath. (Total lines before hook × 2.5s) + intro seconds = time to hook. Must be ≤15s from first sung vocal.

### 2. LYRICS (Imagery? Specificity? Visual/cinematic writing?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Every line earns its place, physical/sensory imagery, show-don't-tell, original phrasing |
| 7-8 | Strong imagery with occasional abstract lines, mostly specific |
| 5-6 | Mix of good and generic, some tell-not-show, functional |
| 3-4 | Mostly generic/abstract, clichés present, diary-trap |
| 1-2 | Entirely generic, no imagery, pure abstraction |

### 3. PROSODY (Stress alignment? Phrase length design? Breath placement?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Every stressed syllable on strong beat, consistent phrase lengths, natural breath points, singable vowels on sustained notes |
| 7-8 | Mostly aligned with 1-2 stuffed lines, generally singable |
| 5-6 | Inconsistent — some lines sing well, others are prose crammed into melody |
| 3-4 | Many lines fight the meter, reversed stresses, breath-hostile |
| 1-2 | Written as prose with no consideration for singing |

**Prosody Check Method:**
- Count syllables per line (target 7-12 at typical BPM)
- Identify stressed syllables — do they land on beats 1 and 3?
- Check for open vowels (ah, oh, aw) on sustained/high notes
- Verify em-dashes create actual breath points
- Flag any line over 12 syllables
- Check for reversed stresses (weak word on strong beat: "the LOVE" instead of "the love OF")

### 4. ARC (Transformation? Turn? Repetition calibrated?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Clear emotional journey, genuine turn in bridge, final chorus means something different than first, V2 adds new information |
| 7-8 | Good movement with one static section or slightly weak turn |
| 5-6 | Some development but largely same emotional state throughout |
| 3-4 | Flat — same feeling start to finish, no transformation |
| 1-2 | No discernible arc |

### 5. STRUCTURE (Length appropriate? Negative space? Contrast?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Perfect length for platform, effective silence/space, dynamic contrast between sections, nothing excess |
| 7-8 | Good structure with one slightly long section or missed contrast opportunity |
| 5-6 | Functional but predictable, some bloat, limited contrast |
| 3-4 | Over-long, no breathing room, or too short to develop |
| 1-2 | Structurally broken |

### 6. ORIGINALITY (Fresh angle? Unique phrasing? Motif development?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Concept, imagery, and phrasing feel genuinely fresh — "I've never heard it said this way" |
| 7-8 | Mostly fresh with one or two familiar phrases |
| 5-6 | Solid but you've heard similar songs explore this territory |
| 3-4 | Relies heavily on common tropes/phrases without subversion |
| 1-2 | Entirely derivative |

### 7. SINGABILITY (Syllable density? Vowel placement? Melodic potential?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Every line sits naturally in the voice, open vowels on holds, consistent phrase rhythm, could be hummed |
| 7-8 | Mostly singable with 1-2 dense passages |
| 5-6 | Mix of singable and prose-like lines, inconsistent |
| 3-4 | Many lines resist melody, consonant clusters, irregular rhythm |
| 1-2 | Reads as prose/poetry, not lyrics |

### 8. COMMERCIAL VIABILITY (Platform fit? Sync ready? 7-second rule?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Hits target platform perfectly, hook in first 15s, right length, sync-placeable |
| 7-8 | Good fit with minor adjustments needed (slightly long, hook slightly late) |
| 5-6 | Works for its context but limited broader appeal |
| 3-4 | Too niche, too long, or structurally uncommercial |
| 1-2 | No commercial pathway |

**⚠️ CONTEXT RULE:** Score relative to INTENDED context. A concept album track scores against concept album standards, not radio singles. For album tracks, "Commercial" means: does this track serve its position in the album's emotional arc, not standalone chart potential.

### 9. GENRE ALIGNMENT (Conventions honored? Fusion balanced?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Perfectly serves genre expectations (Fabbri's 5 rules) while adding freshness |
| 7-8 | Mostly aligned with one genre drift or missed convention |
| 5-6 | General genre fit but doesn't fully commit or honor specific conventions |
| 3-4 | Genre confused — conflicting signals |
| 1-2 | No discernible genre coherence |

**Fabbri's 5 Rules:** Formal (structural conventions), Semiotic (what the genre signifies), Behavioral (performance conventions), Social/Ideological (community values), Juridical/Commercial (marketplace expectations).

### 10. ARRANGEMENT/PRODUCTION (Dynamics? Countermelody? Timbre?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Every production choice signifies correctly (Tagg), dynamic range exceptional, contrast pairs effective, negative space used |
| 7-8 | Strong design with one missed opportunity |
| 5-6 | Functional but predictable, limited dynamic range |
| 3-4 | Production choices don't support the lyric meaning |
| 1-2 | No production consideration |

### 11. ACCENT/VOICE COHERENCE (Dialect? Cultural instrumentation? Vowels singable?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Voice, accent, instrumentation, and mode all from same cultural ecosystem, dialect in lyrics reinforces, persona is clear and distinct |
| 7-8 | Coherent voice with minor inconsistencies |
| 5-6 | Generic voice — correct but not character-driven |
| 3-4 | Voice conflicts with instrumentation or genre |
| 1-2 | No vocal identity consideration |

**Persona distinction (Moore):** The PERSONA is not the singer. Three layers: real person → performer's public identity → song character. The Voice/Accent score assesses whether the SONG CHARACTER's voice is coherent and believable.

### 12. EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE (Ethical stance? Authentic→Transcendent? Responsible?)
| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Expresses without exploiting, offers companionship not solutions, handles darkness responsibly, achieves transcendence, generous to the listener |
| 7-8 | Authentic and thoughtful with one slightly forced moment |
| 5-6 | Genuine but doesn't push beyond surface emotion |
| 3-4 | Manipulative, glorifying, or dismissive of real pain |
| 1-2 | Exploitative or harmful |

**Generosity of spirit (Dodge):** Does the song GIVE something to the listener? Companionship in pain, beauty in darkness, recognition of experience? Or does it only take (attention, emotion, shock value)?

## LAYER 2: ADVANCED ASSESSMENTS (1-10 each)

> These go DEEPER than craft. They assess whether the song is meaningful, spatially designed,
> semiotically correct, artistically ambitious, and temporally rewarding.

### A1. FUNCTIONAL LAYER COMPLETENESS (Moore)

Based on Allan F. Moore's 4 functional texture layers in recorded popular song:

| Layer | What It Is | What to Check |
|---|---|---|
| **Explicit beat** | Percussion/rhythm articulating meter and pulse | Is pulse clear? Does rhythm support or fight the lyric? |
| **Functional bass** | Bass-register instruments providing harmonic foundation | Is harmonic gravity present? Does bass movement support the chord story? |
| **Harmonic filler** | Keyboards/guitars/pads thickening texture and harmonic context | Is the texture appropriate for the genre? Over-thick? Under-supported? |
| **Melodic layer** | Voice + melodic instruments carrying the tune | Is the melody clearly the focal point? Does anything compete with it? |

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | All 4 layers present and perfectly balanced — each serves the song without competing. Layers breathe (thin when needed, full when earned). |
| 7-8 | All layers present, one slightly over/under-represented but song still works |
| 5-6 | One layer missing or competing — texture feels thin or cluttered in places |
| 3-4 | Two layers weak or absent — song feels unmoored or claustrophobic |
| 1-2 | Textural chaos or emptiness — layers fight, overlap, or aren't considered |

**For Suno-generated songs:** Check the Production Direction and instrument list. Are all 4 layers ADDRESSED? A common Suno failure: harmonic filler layer absent (just voice + drums + bass, no mid-range texture).

### A2. SOUNDBOX & PROXEMIC DESIGN (Moore + Lacasse + Moylan)

The **soundbox** is the virtual 3D space of the recording: left-right (stereo), close-far (depth/reverb), high-low (frequency spectrum). **Proxemics** (Lacasse) measures perceived distance between vocalist and listener.

**Proxemic distances:**
| Distance | Production Markers | Emotional Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Intimate** | Dry, close-mic, breath audible, near-whisper | In your ear — secrets, vulnerability, confession |
| **Conversational** | Light room reverb, natural delivery | Same room — storytelling, connection |
| **Social** | Moderate reverb, some projection | Across the room — performance, address |
| **Public** | Heavy reverb, projected, vast space | On a stage/in a cathedral — declaration, anthem |
| **Dissolved** | Underwater/infinite reverb, voice=environment | No boundary — transcendence, transformation |

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Soundbox is vivid and intentional — you can "see" the space. Proxemic distance matches emotional content perfectly. Distance CHANGES meaningfully (e.g., intimate verse → public chorus → dissolved outro). |
| 7-8 | Good spatial design with one section where distance doesn't match emotion |
| 5-6 | Generic space — sounds like "a recording" rather than a specific place |
| 3-4 | Distance contradicts content (intimate lyrics in stadium reverb) |
| 1-2 | No spatial consideration — flat, dimensionless |

**For concept albums:** Track the proxemic arc across the whole album. Does vocal distance tell its own story? (Keeper of the Light: Intimate T1 → Dissolved T8 = transformation arc expressed SPATIALLY.)

### A3. MUSEMIC SIGNIFICATION (Tagg)

Philip Tagg's **museme** = the smallest meaningful musical unit. A museme carries cultural connotations independent of lyrics. The question: **do the sounds MEAN what they should?**

**Process:**
1. Identify the dominant musemes (a riff, a chord movement, a timbre, a rhythmic pattern)
2. Ask: What does this sound CONNOTE culturally? Where else does it appear?
3. Check: Does the connotation MATCH the lyric's intent?

**Examples:**
- Sub-bass pulse → heartbeat monitor, horror, something alive below (CORRECT for T4 Keeper)
- Concertina → coastal folk, warmth, tradition, handmade (CORRECT for T1 Keeper)
- Distorted guitar → aggression, rebellion, electricity (WRONG for a lullaby — unless ironic)
- Glass harmonica → otherworldly, ethereal, membrane between states (CORRECT for T8 Keeper)

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Every timbre, riff, and texture carries the correct cultural connotation. A listener who spoke no English would still FEEL the intended emotion from music alone. |
| 7-8 | Mostly correct with one timbral choice that's neutral (doesn't help or hurt) |
| 5-6 | Some musemes carry mixed signals — instrumentation is "fine" but doesn't SIGNIFY |
| 3-4 | Key musemes contradict lyric intent (upbeat production on grief, chirpy sounds on dread) |
| 1-2 | Complete semiotic mismatch — music and lyrics are saying opposite things |

### A4. SCOPE OF VISION (Dodge et al.)

From the 2025 academic study on universal evaluation criteria. Measures **artistic ambition** — not just craft execution but REACH.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Attempts something genuinely difficult and succeeds — conceptual depth, structural innovation, emotional range, artistic daring. "This couldn't have been easy to write." |
| 7-8 | Ambitious scope with strong execution — reaches beyond convention in at least one dimension |
| 5-6 | Competent but plays it safe — stays within well-mapped territory. No artistic risk. |
| 3-4 | Formulaic — follows templates without adding vision. Could be generated by following a checklist. |
| 1-2 | No discernible artistic intent beyond basic completion |

**Key insight:** A daring song that partially fails may score HIGHER here than a safe song that fully succeeds. This category rewards AMBITION. Pair it with the Core 12 craft scores to get the full picture: high Vision + high Craft = exceptional. High Vision + low Craft = promising but needs work. Low Vision + high Craft = polished but uninspired.

**What counts as "ambitious"?**
- Multi-layered meaning (literal + metaphoric + dramatic irony)
- Structural innovation (non-standard form serving content)
- Emotional range within a single song (not one-note)
- Conceptual depth (requires thought, not just feeling)
- Technical challenges attempted (unusual key, complex prosody, difficult voice)

### A5. SKIP TEST — Dead Zone Scan (A&R Industry)

The ruthless commercial question: **Would a listener reach for the skip button at any point?**

**Method:** Mentally scan every ~15-second window of the song. In each window, is there:
- New information (lyric, melodic, or production)?
- Contrast from the previous window?
- A hook, surprise, or reward for attention?
- Forward momentum toward something?

If ANY window answers "no" to all four questions: that's a dead zone.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Zero dead zones — every moment earns attention. Even "quiet" sections have tension, beauty, or anticipation. |
| 7-8 | One brief moment (~5-8 seconds) that could lose a casual listener, but die-hard fans wouldn't skip |
| 5-6 | One full 15-second dead zone — typically an overly long intro, identical repeated chorus, or unfocused bridge |
| 3-4 | Multiple dead zones — listener would check how much time is left |
| 1-2 | Fundamental engagement failure — song doesn't reward attention from the start |

**Common dead zones to scan for:**
- Intro longer than 15 seconds without sonic interest
- Repeated chorus with identical lyrics AND identical production (no escalation)
- Bridge that restates without turning
- Outro that doesn't resolve or add beauty (just fading repetition)
- Identical pre-choruses with no variation

## LAYER 3: ALBUM-CONTEXT MODULE (Concept Albums Only)

> Skip entirely for standalone songs. For album tracks, these checks verify the song
> serves its narrative, sonic, and structural role within the larger work.

### C1. Position Awareness

Does this track know WHERE it sits in the album's emotional arc?

| Position | What It Should Do |
|---|---|
| Opener (T1) | Establish world, set palette, introduce motifs, create safety/baseline |
| Early (T2-3) | Develop, deepen, introduce first shadows or complications |
| Midpoint | Deliver a pivot, revelation, or escalation point |
| Crisis | Maximum tension, highest stakes, emotional peak |
| Resolution | Resolve, transform, answer questions planted earlier |
| Closer | Return with transformation, provide emotional completion |

**Result:** PASS (serves its position) / CONCERN (partially serves, some mismatch) / FAIL (wrong energy for its position)

### C2. Intention vs Delivery

Compare the Album Blueprint's stated "Core Emotion" for this track against what the lyrics + production ACTUALLY deliver.

**Method:** Read the blueprint's one-line emotion description. Then read the song. Does the song make you feel THAT thing? Or something adjacent? Or something different?

**Result:** MATCH (delivers the stated emotion) / PARTIAL (adjacent emotion, close but not exact) / MISS (different emotion than intended)

### C3. Transition Consciousness

Does the song's ending create gravity toward the next track?

**What to check:**
- Does the final sound relate to the next track's opening sound?
- Is there harmonic pull toward the next key?
- Does the emotional state at the end CREATE desire for what comes next?
- Is there a "thread" (sustained note, motif, silence) that connects?

**Result:** STRONG (clear pull toward next track) / ADEQUATE (ends cleanly, no conflict) / WEAK (just stops, no gravity)

### C4. Dramatic Irony Management

Does the song give the AUDIENCE more information than the CHARACTER has?

**When applicable:** Songs where the character doesn't know the full truth but the listener does (e.g., Maren in T1 doesn't know what the light REALLY keeps, but the album concept tells us).

**What to check:**
- Are ironic lines planted without being on-the-nose?
- Does the irony reward the listener without condescending to the character?
- Is the irony DISCOVERABLE (rewards a second listen within album context)?

**Result:** PRESENT & EFFECTIVE / PRESENT BUT HEAVY-HANDED / ABSENT (note whether it SHOULD be present)

### C5. Motif & Callback Accuracy

Are recurring motifs used correctly per the Album Blueprint?

**What to check:**
- Does the motif appear in the correct tracks (per blueprint)?
- Does the motif carry the correct MEANING at this point in its evolution?
- Are forbidden motifs absent (motifs that don't exist yet in the timeline, or are reserved for other tracks)?
- Is the motif EVOLVED from its last appearance (not just repeated)?

**Result:** CORRECT / MINOR ISSUE (motif present but not evolved) / VIOLATION (wrong track, wrong meaning, or forbidden)

### C6. Sonic Differentiation

Does this track sound sufficiently DIFFERENT from its adjacent tracks?

**Method:** Compare this track's instrument palette against the track before and after it. Estimate overlap percentage. The rule: no two adjacent tracks share >70% palette.

**Result:** PASS (<70% overlap) / BORDERLINE (65-70% overlap — technically passes but feels similar) / FAIL (>70% overlap)

## CALIBRATION & TECHNICAL AUDIT

### Self-Score Delta

If the song includes a self-score (from SOP 01):
- Note the self-composite and the critic composite
- Calculate delta (Critic − Self)
- Self-critiques typically inflate by ~1.0 point
- Delta <0.5 = well-calibrated writer
- Delta 0.5-1.0 = normal inflation
- Delta >1.0 = significant overestimation (or underestimation if negative)

### Parenthetical Layer Audit

If the song uses `()` content in lyrics:
- **Declaration check:** Are named layers (`(robotic layer: text)`, `(echo layer: text)`) declared in the Style Prompt? If not → flag.
- **Density check:** Is layer usage appropriate for the emotional moment? Layers in quiet/vulnerable sections should be sparse. Heavy layering suits climactic moments.
- **Serving vs cluttering:** Do layers ADD meaning (second voice, dramatic effect, production richness) or just ADD NOISE (too many parenthetical interruptions breaking lyric flow)?

### Production-Lyric Alignment (Tagg Applied)

Explicit check: Do production choices REINFORCE or CONTRADICT the lyric meaning?

| Lyric Content | Production Should Be | If Contradicted |
|---|---|---|
| Silence, stillness, absence | Sparse, space, near-silence | Busy production = mismatch |
| Weight, gravity, burden | Heavy bass, slow tempo, dense texture | Light/bright production = mismatch |
| Freedom, release, flight | Open space, bright register, rising | Cramped/heavy production = mismatch |
| Intimacy, confession | Close-mic, dry, small space | Stadium reverb = mismatch |
| Chaos, panic, breaking | Irregular rhythm, dissonance, distortion | Clean/orderly production = mismatch |

**Note:** Intentional contradiction CAN be a valid artistic choice (ironic juxtaposition). If the song deliberately mismatches for effect, note it as "intentional contrast" rather than flagging as error.

### Discovery Depth (Replay Value)

Would a second listen reveal something the first didn't?

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **HIGH** | Multiple hidden layers — a re-listen reveals new lyric meanings, production details, structural callbacks, or dramatic irony invisible on first pass. The song GROWS with familiarity. |
| **MEDIUM** | Some additional texture noticed on re-listen, but main impact is captured first time. |
| **LOW** | What you hear first is what you get. No hidden depth, no rewards for re-listening. |

**What creates high discovery depth:**
- Dramatic irony that only makes sense after hearing later album tracks
- Production details that mirror lyric content (discoverable but not obvious)
- Structural callbacks to other songs (noticeable only with album familiarity)
- Multi-layered metaphors that operate on literal AND symbolic levels
- Subtle lyric variations between repeated sections (easy to miss first time)

## THE 9-STEP ANALYSIS WORKFLOW

Apply this BEFORE scoring (Phase 2 of the critique process — internal scaffolding):

1. **SEMANTIC** — What is the lyric literally about? (subject, characters, setting, actions)
2. **EMOTIONAL** — What is the central affect? Which BRECVEMA mechanisms targeted?
3. **PROSODIC** — Stresses, breaths, rhymes, syllable counts, phrase lengths, singable vowels
4. **NARRATIVE** — Does it escalate, collapse, confess, transform? Where is the turn?
5. **VOICE/ACCENT** — Who is speaking? Vowel placement, rhythm, instrumentation match? (Moore's persona layers: real person → performer → song character)
6. **GENRE MAPPING** — Fabbri's 5 rules satisfied? Fusion balanced (70/30)?
7. **ARRANGEMENT** — Growth pattern, contrast pairs, negative space, repetition calibration. Identify Moore's 4 functional layers.
8. **PRODUCTION** — Timbre palette (Tagg musemes), harmonic choices, mix decisions as meaning, soundbox design (Moylan), proxemic distance (Lacasse)
9. **COMMERCIAL** — Audience, platform, length, sync readiness, 7-second rule, skip test scan

## COMMON FLAG PATTERNS

| Pattern | What To Flag | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Stuffed line | >12 syllables at tempo | Trim or split |
| Reversed stress | Weak word on strong beat | Reorder words |
| Abstract where physical needed | "feeling sad" vs "hands won't stop shaking" | Replace with sensory |
| Rhyme forcing | Meaning bent to serve rhyme | Use slant rhyme or restructure |
| V2 repeats V1 | No new information in second verse | Add narrative progression |
| Bridge restates | Bridge says what chorus already said | Find the TURN |
| Over-explanation | Imagery + thesis statement of same idea | Trust the imagery, cut the thesis |
| Generic hook | Could be in any song | Make specific to THIS song's world |
| Prose bridge | 12+ lines of non-melodic content | Trim to 6-8, impose syllable discipline |
| Soundbox static | Same spatial depth entire song | Vary proxemic distance per section |
| Layer missing | No harmonic filler or no bass presence | Add mid-range texture or bass foundation |
| Musemic contradiction | Bright sounds on dark lyrics | Align timbral connotation to content |
| Dead zone | 15+ seconds with no new information | Add contrast, cut length, or introduce variation |
| Proxemic mismatch | Intimate lyrics at public distance | Match reverb/mic treatment to emotional content |
| Undeclared layer | Named `()` layer without Style Prompt declaration | Add declaration or remove layer |
| Unearned temporal specificity | Time/date that does not anchor, ground, or hook -- just conveys information | Replace with culturally-loaded alternative OR cut |

> **Note:** For full temporal specificity guidelines (6 functions, oddness effect, days-of-week loading, use/avoid criteria), see `core/methodology/songwriting.md` → "Temporal Specificity in Lyrics"

## DECISION GATE (SOP 07 Integration)

The **Core 12-category composite** drives the pipeline decision:
- **≥8.5:** Skip revision → proceed to Optimize (Stage 4)
- **7.0–8.4:** Proceed to Revise (Stage 3)
- **<7.0:** Return to concept with new approach (Stage 1)

**Override rule:** If the core composite passes (≥8.5) but Album-Context checks show FAIL on Position Awareness, Intention vs Delivery, or Motif Accuracy — flag for revision regardless. Album continuity failures override craft scores.

**Advanced composite** is informational — it identifies depth issues that may not surface in craft scoring alone. A song with Core 8.5 but Advanced 6.0 is well-crafted but shallow. A song with Core 7.5 but Advanced 9.0 is ambitious and meaningful but needs craft polish.

## SUNO OPTIMIZATION ASSESSMENT

> Added to every critique. Evaluates whether the Style Prompt, tags, and production choices are
> the BEST creative match for the song's emotional content — not just technically compliant.
> Full reference: `references/SUNO_STYLE_GENRE_REFERENCE.md`

### Purpose

The Suno Optimization Assessment answers: **"Will Suno render this song at its best?"**

It evaluates two things:
1. **Creative optimization** — Are the genre, key, BPM, instruments, and vocal choices the BEST options for this song's emotional content? Could alternatives serve better?
2. **Technical compliance** — Are all required tags present, limits respected, declarations made?

### The 7-Point Dimensional Evaluation

Evaluate each dimension of the Style Prompt against the song's emotional content:

| # | Dimension | Framework Applied | What to Check |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Genre** | Fabbri (5 rules) | Does the genre container satisfy all 5 rules for this song's content? Is it the BEST emotional container? |
| 2 | **Key** | Juslin (cue hierarchy) | Mode is the #1 emotional cue. Does this key carry the correct character for the lyric content? |
| 3 | **BPM** | Genre conventions + emotion mapping | Is tempo correct for BOTH the genre conventions AND the emotional pacing of the lyrics? |
| 4 | **Instruments** | Tagg (musemes) | Does every instrument signify the correct cultural connotation? Any that fight the lyric meaning? |
| 5 | **Vocal style** | Moore (persona) + Fabbri (behavioral) | Does the vocal delivery match genre expectations AND serve the lyrics' emotional arc? |
| 6 | **Era/Production** | Suno era-anchoring science | Is a specific sonic period being invoked? Would era anchoring IMPROVE specificity? |
| 7 | **Space/Mood** | Tagg + Lacasse | Are atmospheric descriptors aligned, non-contradictory, and ≤3 total? |

### Assessment Results

Each dimension gets one of three ratings:
- **CORRECT** — This choice serves the emotional content optimally
- **SUBOPTIMAL** — Fundamentally sound but a better option exists
- **WRONG** — This choice fights the lyric content or violates genre rules

### Overall Verdict

- **OPTIMAL** — All 7 dimensions are CORRECT. No changes needed.
- **COULD IMPROVE** — 1-3 dimensions are SUBOPTIMAL. Alternatives provided.
- **MISMATCHED** — 1+ dimensions are WRONG, or 4+ are SUBOPTIMAL. Revision strongly recommended.

### When to Provide Style Alternatives

If verdict is COULD IMPROVE or MISMATCHED:
- Provide 2 complete alternative Style Prompts (ready to paste into Suno)
- Each with reasoning (why it serves the emotion better) and expected improvement
- Include slider recommendations (Weirdness/Style Influence/Audio Influence)

### Section-Level Tag Evaluation

Beyond the Style Prompt, evaluate per-section production cue tags:
- Does each section's tag signify the correct emotion for that section's lyric content? (Tagg)
- Do tags contrast between adjacent sections? (Variety prevents monotony)
- Are tags ≤3 per section? (Suno parsing sweet spot)
- Do tags support the proxemic arc? (Sparse = intimate, full = public)

### Technical Compliance Checklist

Binary pass/fail checks:
- Required elements present (`[Title:]`, `[Production Direction:]`, `[Vocal Direction:]`, `[end]`)
- Character limits respected (Style ≤1000, Lyrics ≤5000)
- Structural rules followed (section tags on own lines, genre first in Style)
- Declarations made (named `()` layers declared in Style Prompt)
- Tag count in range (5-8 optimal, never >10)
- No contradictory tags present
- Exclusions in correct field
- No artist names in Style Prompt or Production Direction (converted to descriptive production language)

### Key Evaluation Principles

1. **Genre-Emotion Alignment** — The genre must SIGNIFY what the lyrics express. Dark lyrics in an upbeat genre = semiotic violation (Fabbri Rule 2).
2. **Key is King** — Mode is the #1 emotional cue (Juslin). Wrong key = wrong emotion at the deepest level.
3. **BPM serves TWO masters** — Must satisfy genre convention AND emotional pacing simultaneously.
4. **Instruments carry meaning** — Every instrument signifies independently of melody (Tagg). Piano ≠ guitar ≠ synth even playing the same notes.
5. **Era anchoring is more powerful than genre labeling** — Time references give Suno specific sonic periods. "1995 trip-hop" > "dark electronic."
6. **The 70/30 Rule** — Genre combinations: one dominant (70%) + one flavor (30%). 50/50 splits confuse Suno.
7. **5-8 tags maximum** — Past 10, signals conflict and Suno defaults to generic.

## THEORETICAL SOURCES

| Scholar/System | Contribution to This Framework |
|---|---|
| **Allan F. Moore** (*Song Means*, 2012) | 4 functional texture layers, soundbox theory, persona analysis (real/performer/character), interpretation vs analysis distinction |
| **Philip Tagg** (*Music's Meanings*, 2013) | Musemes (smallest meaningful musical units), interobjective comparison, semiotic analysis of popular music, connotation chains |
| **Serge Lacasse** (2000, 2010) | Vocal staging, proxemic interaction in recorded song, perceived distance as meaning |
| **William Moylan** (*Understanding and Crafting the Mix*, 2014) | Recording analysis framework, timbral balance, sound source imaging, ecological listening |
| **Pat Pattison** (Berklee) | Prosody as "appropriate relationship between elements," phrase weight, stable/unstable systems |
| **Franco Fabbri** (1982) | Genre theory — 5 rules of genre membership (formal, semiotic, behavioral, social/ideological, juridical/commercial) |
| **Patrik Juslin** (2013) | BRECVEMA — 8 mechanisms by which music induces emotion (Brainstem reflex, Rhythmic entrainment, Evaluative conditioning, Contagion, Visual imagery, Episodic memory, Musical expectancy, Aesthetic judgment) |
| **Dodge et al.** (2025) | 6 universal criteria for evaluating popular music with inter-rater reliability: Innovation, Beauty, Scope of Vision, Technical Prowess, Generosity of Spirit, Authenticity |
| **Nashville A&R** (industry practice) | Melody memorability, "cut" potential, market fit, 7-second emotional truth test, "would I skip?" test |
| **Dockwray & Moore** (2010) | Establishment of virtual performance space in rock, the "diagonal mix" as norm |
| **Megan Lavengood** (2020, 2021) | Cultural significance of timbre analysis, novelty layer semiotic significance |
| **Juslin & Gabrielsson** (emotional cue research) | Emotional cue hierarchy: mode > tempo > register > dynamics > articulation > timbre. Linearity and additivity of primary cues |
| **Christian Schubart** (1806) | Key characteristics and emotional associations — historical foundation for key-emotion mapping |
| **Suno community research** (2025-2026) | Prompt formula (genre-first, 5-8 tags), era anchoring, genre combination rules, slider behavior, v5 parsing |
