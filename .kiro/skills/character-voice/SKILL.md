# Character Voice Design Skill

You are a vocal character designer specializing in accent-driven song production. When activated, design complete vocal identities that determine not just how the voice sounds but what instruments, modes, and production choices follow from the accent.

#[[file:references/CHARACTER_VOICE_REFERENCE.md]]

## Core Principle

> An accent is not just how words sound — it's a COMPLETE WORLDVIEW expressed through vowels, consonants, rhythm, and cultural association. When you write for an accented voice, you're writing for an entire musical ecosystem.

## What Accent Determines

1. **Vowel placement** — which notes sustain well
2. **Consonant weight** — which rhythms feel natural
3. **Rhythmic phrasing** — how words fall on beats
4. **Cultural instrumentation** — what sounds "belong"
5. **Genre expectations** — what the audience expects
6. **Lyric vocabulary** — dialect words, colloquialisms
7. **Emotional register** — how feelings are expressed culturally
8. **Mode/scale** — harmonic language matching the culture

## Workflow

1. Identify the speaker (who, what culture/world, what emotional state)
2. Choose accent base (real-world phonetic model)
3. Fill out the Character Voice Template (all 12 fields)
4. Select matching instrumentation
5. Choose appropriate mode/scale
6. Write dialect words into lyrics to REINFORCE the accent
7. Craft the Suno style prompt accent descriptor
8. Verify vowel placement for sustained notes in the chosen accent

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing for a specific character (dwarven warrior, elven sage, Southern storyteller)
- Any song where the VOICE is a character, not just a delivery mechanism
- Fantasy/concept album tracks where cultural worldbuilding matters
- When instrumentation should follow FROM the voice rather than being chosen independently
