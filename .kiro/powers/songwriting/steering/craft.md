# Songwriting Craft — Power Steering

## Core Method

Write the chorus FIRST (Nashville method). Every song is a built structure. Prosody is law -- stressed syllables on strong beats. Show don't tell -- imagery over abstraction. The turn changes everything -- a bridge often works best when it creates contrast or a turn. [Tier 2]

## The Hook Test

A hook must be:
- Memorable after ONE listen
- Singable a cappella
- Textable to a friend
- Within 5-30 seconds of first sung vocal [Tier 2] (genre-dependent)
- The emotional peak of the chorus

## Lyric Rules

- Target 7-12 syllables per line (at typical BPM) [Tier 2] -- check stress density and singability for longer lines
- [Tier 2] ABCB rhyme scheme often works well for a conversational feel (one common option, not a universal default)
- Near/slant rhymes > forced perfect
- [Tier 2] V2 usually adds new information or angle (unless reiteration is part of form design)
- No clichés unless subverted
- Imagery = physical, sensory, specific

## The Turn

Every great song has a moment where meaning flips. The bridge often works best when it reveals something new or creates contrast. [Tier 2] The final chorus hits different because of what the bridge said. Design for this.

## Academic Foundations (Critic Uses These)

- **Moore's 4 Layers:** A wide range of popular recordings can be analyzed through explicit beat, functional bass, harmonic filler, melodic layer -- generalized descriptions, not prescriptive rules (not all present in every example)
- **Tagg's Musemes:** Every timbre/riff carries cultural meaning — ensure sounds SIGNIFY correctly
- **Lacasse's Proxemics:** Vocal distance (intimate→public) must match emotional content and CHANGE over the song
- **Moylan's Soundbox:** The mix is a 3D space (stereo, depth, frequency) — design it intentionally
- **Dodge's Vision:** Ambition matters — a daring song that partially fails > a safe song that fully succeeds
- **Skip Test:** If any 15-second window has no new information, contrast, or reward — it's a dead zone
