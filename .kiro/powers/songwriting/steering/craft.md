# Songwriting Craft — Power Steering

## Core Method

Write the chorus FIRST (Nashville method). Every song is a built structure. Prosody is law — stressed syllables on strong beats. Show don't tell — imagery over abstraction. The turn changes everything — bridge must shift meaning.

## The Hook Test

A hook must be:
- Memorable after ONE listen
- Singable a cappella
- Textable to a friend
- Within 15 seconds of first sung vocal
- The emotional peak of the chorus

## Lyric Rules

- 7-12 syllables per line (at typical BPM)
- ABCB default rhyme scheme
- Near/slant rhymes > forced perfect
- Every V2 adds NEW information
- No clichés unless subverted
- Imagery = physical, sensory, specific

## The Turn

Every great song has a moment where meaning flips. The bridge reveals. The final chorus hits different because of what the bridge said. Design for this.

## Academic Foundations (Critic Uses These)

- **Moore's 4 Layers:** Every song has explicit beat, functional bass, harmonic filler, melodic layer — all must be balanced
- **Tagg's Musemes:** Every timbre/riff carries cultural meaning — ensure sounds SIGNIFY correctly
- **Lacasse's Proxemics:** Vocal distance (intimate→public) must match emotional content and CHANGE over the song
- **Moylan's Soundbox:** The mix is a 3D space (stereo, depth, frequency) — design it intentionally
- **Dodge's Vision:** Ambition matters — a daring song that partially fails > a safe song that fully succeeds
- **Skip Test:** If any 15-second window has no new information, contrast, or reward — it's a dead zone
