# SOP 06 Stress Test Report

> **Test Context:** Following SOP 06 step-by-step to design character voices for two characters from Keeper of the Light: (1) The Ancient Thing (non-vocal cosmic entity) and (2) Maren the Keeper (human vocalist).
> **Input:** `references/KEEPER_OF_THE_LIGHT_BIBLE.md` character descriptions
> **Date:** Stress test completed following SOP 06 exactly as written.

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Overall Verdict:** SOP 06 works excellently for its primary use case (human/humanoid singing characters) but has significant gaps for non-vocal entities — characters that are expressed through instrumentation, textures, and production rather than literal voice. The Keeper of the Light album has one such entity (The Ancient Thing, Track 7), which exposed that the SOP's template, dialect step, accent-descriptor step, and verification checklist all assume the character SINGS. For Maren (human vocalist), the SOP is a solid A — every step produces useful output.

**Completion Status:** All 11 steps completable for Maren. For The Ancient Thing, Steps 3-4 required reframing, Step 9 was inapplicable, and Step 10 needed a different template format. Steps 7, 8, and 11 worked well for both characters.

## GENERAL ISSUES

### Issue A: The SOP Assumes All Characters Sing

The title is "Character Voice Design" and every step assumes a VOCAL character. But modern concept albums, dark ambient, and horror genres frequently include entities that are expressed through production, not voice. The Keeper of the Light has one (The Ancient Thing). Fractured Shadows likely has similar non-vocal presences. The SOP needs a non-vocal pathway or clear skip-conditions for non-vocal entities.

### Issue B: The Mode/Scale Table Is Too Limited

Six cultural mappings (Celtic, Nordic, Elvish, Dwarvish, Middle Eastern, Southern US) cover fantasy and regional folk but miss: horror, cosmic, industrial, ambient, and non-cultural tonal choices. These are common in concept albums.

### Issue C: No Mention of Existing Bible Content

If the album bible already has a Character Voice Registry (as the Keeper bible does), the SOP doesn't address the relationship. Are you CREATING a new entry to ADD to the bible? DESIGNING from scratch? The flow should be: "Design the voice using this SOP → format the result to match your bible's Character Voice Registry → add the entry to the bible."

## FINAL VERDICT

**SOP 06 is a solid B+ for vocal characters (Maren: A) and a C+ for non-vocal entities (The Ancient Thing: significant improvisation required).** The core methodology is sound — the progression from identity → phonetics → rhythm → instrumentation → mode → style prompt is logical and produces rich output. The weakness is scope: it's titled "Character Voice Design" but really only handles characters who have A VOICE. The fix isn't a rewrite — it's adding conditional notes and a non-vocal pathway at ~5 key steps. The SOP's FEELING-based approach (Steps 2, 5, 7) already works for non-vocal entities; it's the PHONETIC steps (3, 4, 9, 10) that need non-vocal alternatives.

**Time actually spent:** ~25 minutes for both characters (within the "15-25 minutes per character" estimate for Maren; The Ancient Thing took extra time due to improvisation around inapplicable steps).
