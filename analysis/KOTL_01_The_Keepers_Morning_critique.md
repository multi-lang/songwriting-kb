### COMPOSITE SCORE: 8.4/10

---

## Strongest Line

> "When the world forgets you wait" — This line operates on three simultaneous levels: (1) it's a vulnerable admission of loneliness from a character who otherwise deflects, (2) it carries dramatic irony because the audience will learn she IS waiting on behalf of the world, and (3) rhythmically it lands with seven clean syllables and an open vowel on the final stressed word ("wait"), making it both singable and emotionally resonant. It's the album's thesis compressed into a single breath.

---

## Flagged Issues

1. **Strained syntax in V2 Line 4**
   Current: "The concertina fills what speaks"
   Why: The phrase "fills what speaks" is syntactically awkward — it inverts meaning to serve the rhyme with "weeks." The listener has to decode it rather than feel it. In a song built on clean, physical imagery, this line introduces unnecessary abstraction.
   Fix:
   a) "The concertina speaks for me"
   b) "The concertina breaks the weeks"
   c) "I play until the silence speaks"

2. **Chorus 1 and Chorus 2 are identical — missed escalation opportunity**
   Current: Both choruses use identical lyrics ("the sea won't speak my name"), with only the final chorus shifting to "the sea knows well my name."
   Why: The emotional journey from V1 to V2 introduces isolation and loneliness, but the repeated chorus doesn't reflect this growth. The listener hears the same words with the same meaning twice before the bridge creates the turn — this flattens the middle of the song.
   Fix:
   Introduce a subtle lyric variation in Chorus 2 that reflects V2's loneliness WITHOUT jumping ahead to the bridge's resolution. Example — change C2 Line 4 from "the sea won't speak my name" to "the sea's the only name I speak" — this acknowledges the isolation revealed in V2 while preserving the final chorus's shift as the true resolution.

3. **Pre-chorus repeats identically both times — predictable**
   Current: "And every dawn I climb these stairs / To hold the dark at bay" appears unchanged before both choruses.
   Why: While repetition is a valid folk convention, the song's strength is its internal arc. Having the pre-chorus land identically both times — with no variation in delivery cue, lyric shading, or even a single word swap — makes the structure feel mechanical rather than ritualistic. The second time, she's more isolated (radio off for weeks); the pre-chorus should reflect this.
   Fix:
   Keep Pre-Chorus 1 as-is. For Pre-Chorus 2, vary: "And every dawn I climb these stairs / To hold my dark at bay" — the shift from "the dark" to "my dark" acknowledges the isolation is internal now, not just the literal night. Subtle, but earns its repetition.

---

## Priority Recommendations

1. **Vary Chorus 2 to reflect V2's emotional escalation** — This is the highest-impact fix because it transforms the song's middle from static repetition into a three-stage arc (pride → loneliness-tinged pride → acceptance), making the final chorus's shift land with greater contrast and emotional payoff.

2. **Rework V2 Line 4 for syntactic clarity** — "The concertina fills what speaks" is the only line in the song that requires decoding; replacing it with a cleaner image preserves the otherwise flawless show-don't-tell standard and removes the one moment where the listener's flow breaks.

3. **Introduce one subtractive moment before the bridge** — The arrangement's additive bloom is effective but predictable. Adding a 2-bar drop (e.g., cutting bodhran and guitar to leave only voice + sea-wash for the last two lines of V2) would create a micro-silence that makes the bridge's strip-back feel like arrival rather than repetition of an already-used device.
