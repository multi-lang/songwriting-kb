## Flagged Issues

1. **Tell-not-show throughout (Pattern)**
   Current: "I feel so sad and broken inside my heart tonight" / "The emptiness inside of me is knocking at my door"
   Why: These are thesis statements about an emotion, not demonstrations of it. The listener is TOLD to feel sad rather than being shown something that makes them feel it. Violates the fundamental craft principle of specificity (Pattison).
   Fix:
   a) "My coffee's gone cold for the third time — I haven't moved since five"
   b) "The indent on your side of the bed still holds your shape"
   c) Replace every "I feel X" construction with a physical action or sensory detail that IMPLIES X.

2. **V2 adds zero new information**
   Current: "I feel so sad and broken just like the verse before" (literally self-aware about being repetitive)
   Why: V2's job is narrative progression — new setting, new evidence, time shift, consequence, or escalation. This V2 is structurally identical to V1 and the lyrics acknowledge it. The song stalls here (Pattison: verse must "move the camera").
   Fix:
   a) Shift time: V1 = the breakup night, V2 = weeks later showing what's changed (or hasn't)
   b) Shift perspective: V1 = internal feelings, V2 = external evidence of falling apart (missed calls, unwashed dishes, same clothes three days)
   c) Add a specific event or memory that deepens the pain

3. **Chorus lines too long / stuffed prosody**
   Current: "I'm lost inside the shadows and I've only got myself to blame" (16 syllables)
   Why: At 70 BPM, this line requires either cramming syllables into insufficient beats or extending the line beyond a singable breath. Maximum singable at this tempo is ~10-12 syllables. The line also combines two separate ideas (being lost + self-blame) that compete rather than land together.
   Fix:
   a) "Lost in the shadows — only me to blame" (10 syllables, same meaning)
   b) Split into two lines: "I'm lost inside the shadows / And I've only got myself to blame"
   c) "Shadow-bound and sinking — no one else to blame" (11 syllables, more physical)

4. **Bridge uses platitudes instead of turning**
   Current: "I know that things will get better someday somehow / I know that I'll find my way through this darkness somehow"
   Why: A bridge must SHIFT meaning — reveal, confess, reverse, or deepen. This bridge offers greeting-card optimism that contradicts the established emotional truth without earning the shift. It doesn't tell us WHY things will get better or WHAT has changed. "Somehow" repeated twice signals the writer has no answer, which could be powerful if framed as desperation, but here reads as lazy resolution.
   Fix:
   a) Make the bridge a genuine confession: reveal WHAT caused the breakup/isolation — specific, honest, ugly
   b) Make the bridge a moment of acceptance rather than false hope: "Maybe this is who I am at three AM / Maybe broken wings don't need the sky"
   c) Use the bridge to address someone specific — the person who left, a friend, a past self

5. **Same chord progression for all sections**
   Current: Am - F - C - G throughout the entire song
   Why: Harmonic stasis removes one of the primary tools for creating contrast between sections. Verse, chorus, and bridge should feel different HARMONICALLY, not just dynamically. This is especially critical in a ballad where harmonic movement IS the arrangement (Tagg: chord movement carries semiotic weight).
   Fix:
   — Verse: Am - F - C - G (keep — establishes home)
   — Pre-Chorus: Dm - G - Am - F (creates pull toward chorus)
   — Chorus: F - G - Am - Em or C - G - Am - F (different starting chord = different feel)
   — Bridge: Dm - Em - F - G (rising tension, ascending roots)

6. **Abstract/mixed-metaphor hook**
   Current: "Broken wings of time are falling down on me"
   Why: "Broken wings" = flight imagery. "Of time" = temporal abstraction. "Falling down on me" = gravity/weight. These are three unrelated image systems crammed into one phrase. What ARE broken wings of time? The listener can't picture it. A hook needs to be either instantly graspable (literal) or consistently metaphorical (one image system).
   Fix:
   a) Commit to the wings metaphor: "These broken wings won't catch the wind" (physical, coherent)
   b) Commit to time: "Time keeps falling like a stone on me" (one image, physical weight)
   c) Combine coherently: "Time broke my wings — I'm falling free" (cause and effect, one narrative)

7. **Key/chord contradiction in production notes**
   Current: States "C minor" as key but chords are Am - F - C - G (= A minor / C major)
   Why: This is a factual error that would confuse any session musician or producer. The tonal center of Am-F-C-G is clearly A minor, not C minor. Production notes must be accurate.
   Fix:
   — Change key declaration to "A minor" OR change chords to Cm - Ab - Eb - Bb (actual C minor equivalents)

8. **Identical pre-choruses with no variation**
   Current: Both pre-choruses are word-for-word identical
   Why: Repetition without variation creates dead zones. The second pre-chorus should either add a word change (reflecting V2's progression), a production shift, or a delivery difference. As-is, it's the second dead zone in the song (after V2's non-progression).
   Fix:
   a) PC2 variation: "Every single day I wake up reaching for your side / Every single night the silence screams back twice as wide"
   b) Production fix: Strip PC2 to voice-only (subtractive moment before chorus re-entry)
   c) Cut PC2 entirely — go straight from V2 into chorus for momentum

9. **No bass layer in arrangement (Moore's functional layers)**
   Current: Instruments listed as "Piano, strings, drums" — no bass
   Why: Without functional bass, the harmonic foundation floats. Piano can cover some bass register but needs to be specified. At 70 BPM, a sustained cello bass or piano left-hand bass is essential for grounding the sadness (bass = weight = gravity = emotional seriousness per Tagg).
   Fix:
   — Add: "Cello (low register, sustained, providing bass foundation)" or "Piano left-hand bass, deep and sparse"
   — Consider upright bass entering in chorus for weight contrast

10. **Outro doesn't resolve or add meaning**
    Current: "Broken wings / Broken wings / Broken wings of time"
    Why: Repetition of the hook without transformation, new delivery, or production interest. This is a fade-to-nothing without giving the listener a final emotional landing. The outro should either resolve (completion) or haunt (unresolved beauty).
    Fix:
    a) Final line variant: "Broken wings of time... (whispered) they were always mine"
    b) Production fix: Strip to solo piano playing the progression once, voice trails to nothing — let the SPACE be the ending
    c) Replace with the strongest specific image from a revised song — end on a picture, not an abstraction

## Priority Recommendations

1. **Replace all tell-not-show lyrics with physical/sensory imagery** — This is the fundamental problem. Every "I feel X" must become a visible, hearable, touchable moment. The phone-staring line proves the writer CAN do this. Apply it everywhere. This single change would lift Lyrics from 2→7, Originality from 2→5, and Emotional Intelligence from 4→6.

2. **Rewrite V2 with genuine narrative progression** — V2 must move the camera. New setting, new evidence, time shift, or consequence. The current V2 is an admission of failure. Fix this and Arc jumps from 2→5+, Skip Test improves by at least 2 points.

3. **Trim all chorus lines to ≤12 syllables** — The chorus must be singable at 70 BPM. Cut the current 14-16 syllable lines to 10-12. This fixes Prosody (3→6), Singability (4→7), and makes the hook phrase actually land melodically.

4. **Rewrite the bridge as a genuine emotional turn** — Replace platitudes with specific confession, acceptance, or revelation. This is what transforms the song from "same feeling start to finish" to "a journey." Fixes Arc directly.

5. **Differentiate chord progressions per section** — One progression for the whole song kills contrast. Give each section its own harmonic identity. Fixes Arrangement (3→6) and Genre (4→6) simultaneously.

6. **Fix or replace the hook phrase** — "Broken wings of time" is an incoherent mixed metaphor. Commit to one image system and make it physical. Fixes Hook (3→5+) and Originality.

7. **Add bass layer and specify proxemic design in production notes** — The arrangement needs harmonic grounding and the song needs spatial movement (intimate verse → social chorus → dissolved bridge). Fixes Arrangement and Soundbox scores.
