# Core Methodology: Character Voice

## Purpose

Define the platform-neutral method for designing consistent character voices across songs, albums, and duet or entity-based performances.

## Canonical sources

- `.kiro/sops/06-character-voice-design.md`
- `.kiro/skills/character-voice/SKILL.md`
- `references/CHARACTER_VOICE_REFERENCE.md`
- `MUSIC_PRODUCTION_THEORY.md`

## Core workflow

1. Identify who is singing or speaking and why they are the right voice for the song.
2. Define register, vocabulary, accent/dialect if relevant, emotional state, and vocal delivery.
3. Map non-vocal identity markers such as instrumentation, mode, rhythmic behavior, and production space.
4. Apply voice constraints during drafting so character identity shapes line choice, not only final performance notes.
5. Verify the character voice against album continuity when the song belongs to a concept album.

## Testing/validation

- Character-driven songs should include voice notes before final output.
- Album character voices should be compared against the album bible or character reference.
- Duets and multi-character songs should label voice changes clearly enough for rendering and critique.
